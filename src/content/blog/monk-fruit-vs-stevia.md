Natural sweeteners have become non-negotiable for clean-label supplement brands. The two dominant options — monk fruit extract and stevia — each bring distinct advantages and trade-offs that affect formulation, cost, and consumer perception.

## Taste Profile

**Monk fruit** (Luo Han Guo) delivers sweetness primarily through mogrosides, particularly mogroside V. The taste is clean with minimal aftertaste, though some consumers detect a slight fruity or caramel-like note at higher concentrations.

**Stevia** sweetness comes from steviol glycosides, with Reb A being the most common. Stevia is notorious for a bitter, licorice-like aftertaste — especially in formulations that rely too heavily on it. Newer high-purity Reb M and Reb D extracts have significantly reduced this issue but come at a premium.

## Cost Comparison

This is where the decision gets real:

- **Stevia (Reb A 97%)**: $80–120/kg
- **Stevia (Reb M 95%)**: $300–500/kg
- **Monk Fruit (50% mogroside V)**: $150–250/kg

For a typical protein powder serving, monk fruit adds roughly $0.03–0.05 per serving in sweetener cost, while standard stevia comes in at $0.01–0.02. The gap narrows significantly if you're using premium Reb M stevia.

## Formulation Considerations

- Monk fruit works best when paired with erythritol or allulose for bulk sweetness
- Stevia Reb A needs careful dosing — overshoot and the bitterness compounds
- Both are heat-stable for processing, but monk fruit is more pH-stable across formulations
- Blending both (70% monk fruit / 30% stevia Reb M) can optimize cost while maintaining clean taste

## Regulatory Status

Both monk fruit extract and high-purity stevia extracts are GRAS. They appear on supplement facts panels as "Monk Fruit Extract" and "Stevia Leaf Extract" (or "Stevia rebaudiana") respectively. Neither triggers major allergen declarations.

> The trend is clearly moving toward monk fruit. Consumer surveys consistently show higher preference for monk fruit on ingredient labels, even when taste differences are minimal in blind testing.

### Bottom Line

If budget allows, monk fruit delivers a cleaner taste and stronger label appeal. If cost is the primary constraint, Reb A stevia works but requires careful formulation to manage aftertaste. The best approach for most products: a monk fruit-forward blend with a small stevia component to manage cost.
